What It's Costing You

Reviews Aren't a "Nice to Have." They're a P&L Line.

97% of consumers read reviews before choosing a local business, and they read about 10 of them, spending 13–14 minutes, before they'll trust you. 57% won't even consider a business under 4 stars, and the share demanding 4.5+ is climbing. Old, sparse, or negative reviews actively repel the customers actively looking for you — and 74–85% of consumers consider a review older than three months irrelevant, so a pile of stale five-stars doesn't save you.

The core insight: only ~0.5–1.2% of customers review unprompted, but 69% review when simply asked. Most owners forget to ask, and when they do it's maybe 5% of customers. Meanwhile only ~5% of businesses respond to reviews while 89% of consumers expect a response. Every one of those gaps is revenue: review signals are ~15–17% of Google's local ranking weight, and the Local Pack drives 126% more traffic and 93% more calls than positions 4–10. Automation is what closes all of it — at scale, on autopilot.
