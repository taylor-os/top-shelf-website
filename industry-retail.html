Meet Maple & Main Mercantile

Dana owns a specialty gift and home-goods boutique on a small-town main street. She hand-picks every candle, card, and ceramic, and her regulars love the curated feel — but she's a one-woman show behind the counter, and everything from marketing to inventory lives in her head.

Sound familiar? Dana isn’t doing anything wrong — the work is great. The money leaks somewhere else: the calls nobody answers, the quotes that go cold, the reviews that never get asked for. Here’s exactly where it’s going, what it’s costing, and what we’d do about it.

Where the money leaks

Six Leaks Costing Retail & Local Owners Every Week

Every one of these is a problem we can actually fix — and every fix links to how we do it. Follow the thread as far as you want.

They browse with you, then buy on their phone

A customer spends fifteen minutes with you talking through two candles, snaps a photo of the label, thanks you warmly, and leaves empty-handed. That night the same candle shows up on her doorstep from an online seller.

Showrooming and webrooming: shoppers use your store to see, touch, and get advice, but the actual purchase happens online where a price or a link is one tap away. When you have no online presence of your own, that sale simply leaks to a marketplace. Every 'let me think about it' that turns into an online order is full-margin revenue you did the work to earn and then handed to a competitor who never met the customer.

81%of retail shoppers do online research before buying, and over half prefer to see a product in-store before purchasing it online. helplama.com

How Top Shelf fixes it: We put your shop where those searches land — a fast website with your real inventory and a 'buy it here' path, plus local SEO so your store shows up first when someone researches what you sell. When they look you up after leaving, they find you, not Amazon. More on Websites & Local SEO →

You get: A branded website tied to your products and a local search presence that captures the research-then-buy shopper before the sale drifts online.

The register goes dark the moment you lock up

You flip the sign to Closed at 6, and for the next sixteen hours your shop earns nothing — while your customers are home on the couch, phones in hand, ready to spend.

With no online store, your only sales window is the hours the door is unlocked. Evenings, Sundays, holidays, and the after-dinner scroll — when a lot of buying actually happens — are dead time for your revenue. You're paying rent and stocking product 24/7 but only selling a third of the day. The gift someone wants to grab at 9pm goes to whoever has a checkout button open.

25-30%of daily online order volume comes in the evening hours, after most physical shops have closed for the day. surebright.com

How Top Shelf fixes it: We stand up an online store that mirrors your shelves, so your best sellers keep selling while you sleep — local pickup and shipping, tied to the same inventory so you never oversell. More on Online Store →

You get: A working e-commerce storefront that turns your closed hours into a second revenue stream, with pickup and shipping options.

You can't see the shelf, so it bleeds money quietly

You go to grab a customer the last of a popular item and find the spot empty — but your gut said you had three. Meanwhile a whole shelf of last season's stock is just sitting there, tying up cash you could've reordered winners with.

Inventory blind spots: no real count, no low-stock alerts, no read on what's dead weight versus what's flying. Combine that with shrinkage from theft and mis-scans, and product value evaporates without you ever seeing the line item. Dead stock freezes your cash, stockouts hand sales to the shop down the street, and shrink skims your margin off the top — all invisible until you're short at the end of the month.

$112.1Bin retail shrink in 2025 (about 1.4% of sales on average) — losses from theft, error, and mismanaged stock most owners can't even see. spot.ai

How Top Shelf fixes it: A modern POS and inventory system gives you a live count, low-stock and reorder alerts, and reports that flag dead stock and shrink patterns — so the shelf you can't physically watch is watching itself. More on POS & Inventory →

You get: Real-time inventory tied to your register, reorder alerts, and dead-stock and shrinkage reporting so nothing bleeds out unseen.

When someone searches what you sell, a chain shows up instead of you

A shopper two blocks away types 'gift shop near me' into her phone. Your unclaimed, half-empty Google listing sits below three big-box stores — so she drives right past your window to one of them.

A weak or unmanaged Google Business Profile and thin local search presence means you're invisible at the exact moment a ready-to-buy neighbor is looking for what you carry. Wrong hours, no photos, no reviews showing = no click. These aren't tire-kickers — near-me searchers are minutes from buying. Every one that lands on a competitor's listing is a walk-in you should have had, gone for good.

76%of people who run a 'near me' search visit a business within 24 hours, and 28% of local searches end in a purchase. biziq.com

How Top Shelf fixes it: We claim and fully build out your Google Business Profile — hours, photos, categories, posts — and tune your local SEO so you rank in the map pack for what you actually sell. We also make it dead-simple to keep fresh reviews flowing. More on Reviews & Reputation →

You get: A fully optimized Google Business Profile and local search presence that puts your shop in front of nearby, ready-to-buy shoppers.

First-time buyers leave and you have no way to bring them back

A tourist buys eighty dollars of your candles, gushes about the shop, and walks out the door forever. You have no name, no email, no phone number — no way to ever reach her again.

No loyalty engine and no captured contacts. Every sale is a stranger you have to win from scratch, instead of a relationship that compounds. Your best asset — the people who already love the place — is walking out unlogged. You're spending to attract new faces while the proven buyers slip away. It costs far more to land a new customer than to bring a happy one back for a second and third visit.

up to 40%more spend, on average, from loyalty program members versus non-members — and repeat customers spend roughly 3x more per visit than first-timers. capitaloneshopping.com

How Top Shelf fixes it: We set up a simple points-and-rewards loyalty program at your register that captures each customer as they check out — turning one-time buyers into regulars who come back for the reward and spend more when they do. More on Memberships & Loyalty →

You get: A points-and-rewards loyalty program that captures customers at checkout and gives them a reason to keep coming back.

You're not collecting contacts, so you can't fill a slow week

It's the third dead Tuesday in a row and you'd love to text your regulars 'new arrivals just hit, come by this weekend' — but you don't have a single phone number or email on file to send it to.

No customer list means no way to create demand on command. Seasonal cash-flow swings and quiet stretches hit hard because you have no direct line to the people who already buy from you — you just wait and hope. Slow seasons stay slow, new arrivals sit unseen, and you're at the mercy of walk-in luck instead of being able to summon traffic with one message when you need cash flow most.

$45in retail revenue per $1 spent on email marketing — and SMS plus email together drives about 56% higher ROI than email alone. omnisend.com

How Top Shelf fixes it: We build automatic contact capture into checkout and loyalty, then set up email and SMS campaigns you can fire in a click — new arrivals, a slow-week promo, a holiday push — so you can steady the swings and drive traffic on demand. More on Marketing & Social →

You get: A growing customer email and SMS list plus ready-to-send campaigns to fill slow weeks and smooth out seasonal dips.
