Where Your Revenue Is Going

Five Leaks Quietly Draining Local Shops

You’re Invisible in the Search That Sends Foot Traffic

When someone searches “boutique near me” or “gift shop [your town],” Google shows a Map Pack of three. If you’re not in it, you effectively don’t exist for that shopper — and ~76% of them visit a store within 24 hours. That’s ready-to-buy demand, in your ZIP code, handed to whoever ranks. An incomplete or unclaimed Google Business Profile is the single most common reason local shops lose it.

~76% of local searchers buy within a day

Your Website Is Dated — Or You Don’t Have One

Over half of local searches happen on a phone, and shoppers judge your whole store by the site in about 3 seconds. A slow, cramped, or nonexistent website reads as “closed” or “not for me.” No hours, no directions, no product feel — and they bounce to the shop that looks open and cared-for. Roughly 40% of visitors leave a page that takes more than 3 seconds to load.

~40% leave a slow site instantly

The Chain Down the Street Has 400 Reviews. You Have 22.

~88% of people trust online reviews as much as a friend’s recommendation, and review volume, recency, and rating are top local-ranking factors. A thin, stale review count doesn’t just look risky to shoppers — it buries you beneath competitors in the Map Pack. Most happy customers would leave a review if asked; unprompted, only about 1 in 100 ever do.

~1% review you without being asked

No Repeat-Customer Engine — Every Sale Is a Stranger

A repeat customer spends more, buys more often, and costs nothing to re-acquire — existing shoppers convert far more readily than a first-timer, and even a small lift in retention can lift profit meaningfully. But with no email, SMS, or loyalty program, every great sale walks out the door and you never hear from them again. You’re paying to fill a bucket with a hole in the bottom.

Repeat buyers = your cheapest revenue, unused

The Walk-Ins You Never Capture (or Follow Up)

Someone browses for ten minutes, loves a piece, says “I’ll think about it,” and leaves. You have no name, no number, no way to bring them back — so you don’t. Most stores never capture the shopper at the counter and never send a single follow-up. That interested browser was one text away from a sale, and you let them evaporate.

Interested browsers lost to zero follow-up
How We Fix It

Five Systems That Fill Your Store

A Fast, Mobile-First Site + Local SEO That Wins the Map Pack

We build a clean, phone-first website that loads instantly and shows what matters — hours, directions, products, and your story — then optimize your Google Business Profile and local listings so you surface for every “near me” search in your area.

Result: You go from invisible to top-3 in the Map Pack — the exact spot ~76% of local searchers act on within a day. Ready-to-buy demand in your ZIP finally lands on you instead of the chain down the street.

An Automated Reviews Engine That Builds Trust on Autopilot

After a purchase, a review request goes out automatically by text or email — at the perfect moment, when the customer is happiest. No awkward asking at the counter, nothing for your staff to remember. Steady, fresh reviews accumulate month after month.

Result: ~88% of shoppers trust reviews like a friend’s word. Going from a thin, stale count to a steady stream of fresh 5-star reviews lifts both your Map Pack ranking and the confidence of every person deciding whether to make the trip.

CRM + Email, SMS & Loyalty That Bring Shoppers Back

We capture the customer at the counter and put them into a system that stays in touch — new-arrival texts, birthday offers, a simple loyalty program, and win-back campaigns for people who haven’t visited in a while. Your best customers hear from you again.

Result: You stop treating every sale as a stranger. Repeat customers are your cheapest, most profitable revenue — and a small lift in retention compounds into a meaningfully bigger bottom line, without spending a dollar more on ads.

POS & Inventory So You Finally Know Your Numbers

A modern point-of-sale that tracks every sale, every SKU, and every customer — so you know your best sellers, your dead stock, your busiest hours, and your real margin. Reorder before you sell out; stop tying up cash in product that doesn’t move.

Result: You buy smarter, mark down less, and never lose a sale to an empty shelf. The guesswork disappears, and the data feeds straight into your loyalty and marketing so the whole system works together.

Marketing That Fills Your Slow Days

Local social posts, seasonal promotions, and targeted offers to your customer list — timed to move the days that need moving. A slow Tuesday becomes a flash sale to your loyalty members; a new shipment becomes an event people show up for.

Result: Your calendar stops having dead zones. Instead of hoping foot traffic shows up, you create it — pulling proven customers back in on the days you choose, with offers that actually get them off the couch.

Common Questions

What Shop Owners Ask Before They Start

I.

I already have a Google listing. Isn’t that enough?

Rarely. Most listings are unclaimed or half-filled — wrong hours, no photos, missing categories, thin reviews — which is exactly why they don’t rank. We optimize the profile, fix your listings across the web, and build a steady review flow so you actually land in the top-3 Map Pack, not page two.

II.

Do you work with my existing POS, or do I have to switch?

Either way. If you have a POS you like, we integrate the reviews, CRM, and marketing around it. If your current setup is holding you back, we can move you to a modern system — but we never force a switch you don’t need. You own everything either way.

III.

I’m a one-person shop — will this be one more thing to manage?

The opposite. Review requests, follow-ups, and offers run automatically once we set them up. You keep serving customers; the system quietly asks for the review, texts the new arrivals, and books the slow-day event. You review a simple dashboard, nothing more.

IV.

Do I own my website and everything you build?

Yes — all of it. Your website, your phone number, your customer list, your reviews, and your Google profile. Everything we build or set up is yours to keep, always.
