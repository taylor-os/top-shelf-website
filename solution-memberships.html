What’s inside

How Memberships & Loyalty Works for You

Memberships / Recurring Service Plans

A monthly or yearly plan that keeps customers on the books and money coming in between jobs.

Why it matters: Every month you start from zero, hustling for the next job with no idea what next week looks like. Great customers drift off and forget about you until something breaks, and by then they might call someone else. One-and-done customers mean unpredictable income and a business that never feels safe.

3-5xThe average subscription customer generates 3-5x more revenue over their lifetime than a one-time customer. swell.is
You get: Predictable monthly income, customers who stick around, and a business worth far more when you sell it.

Loyalty & Rewards Program

A simple rewards system that gives your best customers a reason to keep coming back to you.

Why it matters: You do great work, then the customer forgets you the second the job's done. There's no reason for them to pick you over the next guy's coupon, so you keep paying to win back people you already earned. It costs far more to find a new customer than to keep one you already have happy.

4.8xCompanies with strong loyalty programs see an average ROI of about 4.8x, and 90% report positive ROI. netguru.com
You get: Repeat customers who spend more and come back more often, without you discounting yourself to death.

Referral Program

A rewarded system that turns your happy customers into a steady stream of new ones.

Why it matters: Your best customers would gladly recommend you, but there's no easy way for them to do it, so they don't. That word-of-mouth, your cheapest and best lead source, just evaporates while you spend on ads instead. Referred customers are your highest-quality, lowest-cost leads, and you're leaving them on the table.

4xReferred customers convert 4x better than customers acquired through paid channels. growsurf.com
You get: A reliable flow of pre-sold, easy-to-close new customers, without paying for another ad.

Gift Cards

Branded gift cards your customers can buy for friends and family, putting money in your pocket today.

Why it matters: Customers would happily gift your service to a neighbor or grown kid, but you've got no way to sell it, so that easy money and that brand-new customer both walk right past your door. A gift card is cash up front today plus a new customer who almost always spends more than the card's worth.

75%About 75% of gift card recipients spend more than the card's value, adding an average of $59 per purchase. schedulingkit.com
You get: Money in the bank now, new faces at the door, and some cards that never even get redeemed.
