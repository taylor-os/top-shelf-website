Where the Money Is Going

Five Leaks Draining the Average Shop

These aren't small inefficiencies. A single mid-size shop realistically leaks $50K–$150K a year across missed calls, deferred work, and no-shows — and a single-rooftop dealer leaks into seven figures. The cost of fixing it is a rounding error against that.

📞
The Phone Is the Business — and It's Bleeding
$49,000–$135,000/year in missed-call revenue
Up to 21–24% of calls to auto service businesses go unanswered during business hours — and independent-garage analyses put it as high as 38%. When the phone isn't answered, ~60% of callers won't wait on hold more than a minute; they don't leave a voicemail, they just call the next shop on Google. Worse: 56–60% of calls arrive after hours, when your door is locked and you're invisible to more than half your potential customers. A shop missing ~10 calls a week, converting half at a $400 average ticket, forfeits more than $40,000 a year. Higher-volume shops lose ~$135,000. Dealerships miss 60% of service calls at peak — up to $1M+ a year per store.
🎯
Leads Come In — and Fall Through the Cracks
43.2% of dealership sales leads mishandled; 14.1% never logged at all
The 2025 Foureyes benchmark found 43.2% of dealership sales leads are mishandled — missed calls, never entered in the CRM, lapsed follow-up. 65% of returning web leads don't hear back within 24 hours; 61.6% aren't contacted for 8+ days — yet 60% of buyers purchase within 3 days and 78% buy from the first business that responds. Respond in under 5 minutes and you're up to 21–100x more likely to convert. Most shops still measure response time in hours to days while the window is minutes. Every hour of delay is measurable lost gross.
⭐
14 Reviews vs. the Competitor's 340
Businesses at 4.0★+ appear in the local pack 58% more often
98% of consumers read reviews for local businesses and 68% won't consider a business rated below 4 stars. Review quantity, recency, and response rate are direct Google ranking signals — a shop with 200 fresh reviews consistently outranks one with 40 stale ones. If you're not in the top-3 map pack, you're effectively invisible to the 70%+ of local searchers who never scroll past it. Map-pack shops get 126% more traffic and 93% more calls than the shops on page two. Your competitor with 340 reviews isn't better than you — they just asked.

Deferred Work Nobody Follows Up On
~$4,200/month in recoverable declined-service revenue
You quote the brakes. The customer says "next month." Then it's forgotten — pure margin left on the table. Recovering declined-service rate from ~11% to ~24% was worth ~$4,200/month extra in one cited example, at an average deferred ticket of ~$280. And digital vehicle inspections (photo/video the customer approves from their phone) lift average repair order from $612 to $741 (+21%) — one documented shop went from $583 to $1,000+. Many shops own the feature and never use it, chasing approvals by phone tag instead.

Idle Bays & No-Shows
$20,000–$25,000/month per idle bay; 15–20% no-show rate
An empty service bay costs $20,000–$25,000 a month in lost billable hours, and the average shop runs a 15–20% appointment no-show rate — pegged at roughly $30,000/year in scheduling friction for a typical shop. Meanwhile the technician shortage is the #1 stated pain (31% of owners name it their top challenge; 88% say hiring is hard), which makes every wasted labor hour chasing parts, phone tag, and re-quoting even more expensive. You can't hire your way out — you have to plug the leaks.
