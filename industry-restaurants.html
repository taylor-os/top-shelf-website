Where the money leaks

Six Leaks Costing Restaurants & Bars Owners Every Week

Every one of these is a problem we can actually fix — and every fix links to how we do it. Follow the thread as far as you want.

The phone won't stop ringing — and every missed call walks next door

It's 6:45 on a Friday. You've got a full dining room, three tickets on the rail, and the phone is ringing for the fourth time. You can't grab it — you're plating. By the time you look up, it's stopped. That was probably a party of six trying to book.

Your busiest hours are exactly when nobody's free to answer the phone. Reservation calls, takeout orders, and 'are you open?' questions all pile up during the rush and go straight to nobody. The people who called don't leave a voicemail — they call the restaurant down the street. Every unanswered call at dinner is a table or an order you'll never know you lost.

85%of callers whose call goes unanswered won't call back — they simply move on to the next restaurant, and 60-70% of inbound restaurant calls have order or reservation intent. ringfoods.com

How Top Shelf fixes it: We put an AI phone receptionist on your line that answers every call in your restaurant's voice — instantly, even mid-rush. It books reservations, takes takeout orders, answers hours-and-menu questions, and texts you anything that needs a human. No call ever rings out again. More on AI Phone & Receptionist →

You get: Every call answered on the first ring, reservations and orders captured while you're on the floor, and your hands free to run service instead of the phone.

The delivery apps are eating 30 cents of every dollar

A $60 order comes through DoorDash. You cook it, box it, hand it off — and by the time the commission, service, and marketing fees come out, you've cleared almost nothing. You're doing the work and they're taking the margin.

Third-party delivery apps charge 15-30% commission per order, and once every add-on fee is counted the real bite often lands closer to 30-40%. On a business that already runs thin, that's most or all of the profit on those orders. At 30% commission a well-run kitchen is left with roughly 0-2% margin on a delivery order — you're covering costs and not much else, while the app owns your customer's name, phone, and reorder.

0-2%profit margin left on a delivery order at 30% commission — the fees can wipe out the entire margin on a typical restaurant order. rezku.com

How Top Shelf fixes it: We build your own commission-free online ordering right on your website — same convenience for the customer, but the order, the margin, and the customer's contact info stay with you. Keep the apps for reach if you want; steer your regulars to order direct. More on Online Booking →

You get: A branded 'Order Now' button on your site, orders that don't cough up 30% to a middleman, and a customer list that's finally yours.

You held the table. They never showed.

You blocked your best four-top for an 8 o'clock reservation on a Saturday. They don't show, don't call. That table sat empty through your peak turn while you were turning walk-ins away at the door.

No-shows run around 5-7% on a normal night and can top 20% on holidays and weekends when there's no reminder and no card on file. Each one is a prime-time seat that earns you nothing. The restaurant industry loses an estimated $16 billion a year to no-shows. For one restaurant, even a handful a week adds up to tens of thousands of dollars in empty prime-time seats over a year.

up to 65%reduction in no-shows when a card is required to hold the reservation, and automated reminders can cut no-shows dramatically on their own. blog.tobeout.com

How Top Shelf fixes it: We set up online booking with automatic text and email reminders, optional card-hold or deposit for peak times, and one-tap cancel so a freed table can go back out to your waitlist instead of sitting dark. More on Online Booking →

You get: Automatic confirmations and reminders, fewer empty prime-time seats, and a waitlist that fills cancellations for you.

Your star rating is quietly deciding how many people walk in

A couple two blocks away is picking dinner on their phone. Yours and the spot down the street both look good — but they've got 4.6 stars and 900 reviews, and you're sitting at 4.1 with your last review from five months ago. They tap the other one. You never even had a shot.

Diners filter by rating and recency before they ever consider your food. A stale, lower rating isn't a vanity number — it's a filter that removes you from consideration before anyone reads the menu. A Harvard Business School study found a one-star increase in rating drives a 5-9% increase in revenue — controlling for location, cuisine, and price. The reverse is just as real: a lagging rating costs covers every single night.

5-9%revenue increase from a one-star increase in online rating, per a Harvard Business School study of restaurant reviews. akuting.com

How Top Shelf fixes it: We automatically invite happy guests to leave a review right after they dine, route any grumbles to you privately first, and help you reply to every review fast — so your rating climbs and your reviews stay fresh and recent. More on Reviews & Reputation →

You get: A steady stream of new 5-star reviews, a rating that keeps you in the running, and every review answered without you living in your inbox.

They looked you up, couldn't find the menu, and picked somewhere else

Someone's deciding where to take a first date. They Google you, land on a site that loads slow and shows last spring's menu, can't tell if you're even open tonight, and bail. You never knew they were there.

Nearly everyone checks your menu and hours online before deciding — and an outdated, hard-to-read, or wrong-hours site tells them you might be closed, sloppy, or gone. Old info costs you the customers you never see. 93% of people check the online menu before visiting, 30% are put off by a website that looks old or out of date, and most diners will skip a place entirely if they can't find the menu online. That's demand walking away silently.

93%of diners check the online menu before visiting a restaurant, and 30% are discouraged by a website that looks outdated. get.popmenu.com

How Top Shelf fixes it: We build a fast, mobile-first website with a menu that's easy to update, correct hours everywhere it counts, and local SEO so you actually show up when someone nearby searches 'dinner near me' or your cuisine plus your town. More on Websites & Local SEO →

You get: A site that loads fast and looks current, a menu and hours you can keep accurate in seconds, and higher placement when hungry locals search.

Your regulars are your paycheck — and you have no way to reach them

It's a rainy Tuesday and the dining room is dead. You know a couple hundred people love this place and would come in tonight if they just remembered you exist — but you have no list, no number, no way to say 'get in here, first round's on us.'

Most of your money comes from people who've already been in. But without a list and an easy way to reach them, filling a slow weeknight or turning a first-timer into a regular is left to luck. 60-80% of restaurant sales come from repeat guests, yet around 70% of first-time diners never return. Every first-timer you don't capture is a regular you'll never have — and every slow night you can't fill is fixed cost with no revenue.

60%of restaurant revenue is driven by repeat guests, while roughly 70% of first-time diners never return. olo.com

How Top Shelf fixes it: We set up a loyalty program and email/SMS list that grows on its own from bookings and orders, then send targeted offers to fill slow dayparts — a Tuesday special, a 4pm 'last-minute table' text, a win-back to guests who've gone quiet. Texts get read (98% open rate) so a slow night gets a same-day fix. More on Memberships & Loyalty →

You get: A growing list of your own customers, loyalty perks that pull first-timers back, and a one-tap way to fill a dead Tuesday tonight.
