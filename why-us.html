The Problem With the Other Guys

Lock-In, Opacity, and Hostage Assets

The big all-in-one marketing companies built their business model on friction. They lock you into long contracts because they know their results won't keep you on their own. They build your website on their platform — so the site, the domain, and sometimes even your Google profile technically belong to them. When you decide to leave, you don't leave with anything. You start over from scratch, and they're betting that's painful enough that you'll just keep paying.

Then there's the reporting. You get a dashboard full of impressions, sessions, and vanity metrics that never answer the only question that matters: did this make me money? The jargon isn't an accident — it's cover. If you can't tell whether it's working, you can't tell whether to cancel. We think that's backwards. You should be able to see exactly what every dollar does, own everything we build, and stay only because it's working.
