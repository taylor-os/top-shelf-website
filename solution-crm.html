What It’s Costing You

The Fortune Is in the Follow-Up

Only 27% of leads ever get contacted at all — up to 73% are never reached. 80% of new leads never become customers, and the number-one reason is poor follow-up: late responses, no nurturing, dropped conversations. Nearly half of salespeople never follow up after the first touch, yet 80% of sales need five or more. You paid — in ads, time, or referrals — to make the phone ring, then let most of those people walk because nobody chased them.

And your best future customers are the ones who already paid you once. Selling to an existing customer closes 60–70% of the time versus 5–20% cold, and reactivating them is 5–7x cheaper than buying a new lead with ads. Most businesses have a goldmine of past customers and dead quotes sitting in a spreadsheet — or worse, in one employee’s phone that walks out the door when they quit. A CRM turns all of it into a single owned asset that works for you automatically.

What We Do

Catch Every Lead. Follow Up on Autopilot.

Every capability below answers one question: and here’s what that gets you.

Instant Lead Response (Speed-to-Lead)

The second a lead comes in — form, Google ad, Facebook, webchat — the CRM auto-texts and emails them within seconds, 24/7. While your competitor’s lead sits in a voicemail box overnight, yours already got a text back and booked.

More money: responding in under 5 minutes makes you 21x more likely to win the lead; 78% of buyers buy from whoever answers first. More customers: you capture the after-hours and lunch-rush leads competitors let go cold. Automation: zero human effort, nights and weekends included.

Missed-Call Text-Back & Unified Inbox

Miss a call? The CRM instantly texts “Sorry we missed you — what can we help with?” And every channel — calls, SMS, email, webchat, Facebook, Instagram, Google messages — flows into one conversation thread per customer.

More money: recovers a slice of the ~$126K/yr the average SMB loses to missed calls. More customers: stops the 62% of unanswered callers who otherwise call a competitor. Less headache: nothing gets missed across five apps ever again.

Automated Follow-Up Sequences

Every new lead and every open quote drops into a multi-touch text and email sequence — day 0, day 1, day 3, day 7 — that runs until they book or say no.

More money: recovers deals from the ~73% of leads that get zero or one touch; 80% of sales need 5+ follow-ups and now they get them. Automation: persistence runs itself. Lower overhead: frees hours a week of manual “just checking in” chasing.

Database Reactivation Campaigns

The CRM holds every past customer and dead quote, then runs automated “we miss you / time for service / here’s an offer” SMS and email campaigns to the whole list.

More money — the biggest hidden lever: past customers close 60–70% vs. 5–20% cold. A 3,000-contact database at a 5% reactivation rate and $2,500 avg ticket = ~$375K recovered. Lower overhead: 5–7x cheaper than new leads. Repeat business from a list that used to die in a spreadsheet.

Visual Pipeline, Booking & Review Automation

A drag-and-drop board of every open opportunity and its dollar value, self-booking with automated reminders, and an auto-review-request text 24–48 hours after every job — all on one owned platform.

More money: no hot quote goes cold, fewer no-shows, and 3–5x more Google reviews within 90 days. More reviews → higher ranking → more inbound leads → more jobs → more reviews. Less headache: one source of truth the business owns, not a departing employee.

An all-in-one CRM runs about $97–$300/mo. One recovered lead, saved missed call, or reactivated customer is worth $150–$2,500+. One customer a month pays for it — the CRM saves you dozens.
